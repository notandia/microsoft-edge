# Competitive benchmark: Lazy Scholar 3.0

Lazy Scholar is a substantial direct competitor, not an obsolete predecessor. Its current product advertises reference extraction, DOI verification, retractions, expressions of concern, corrections, PubPeer discussions, Beall-list venue warnings, preregistration checks, citation metrics, document navigation, and reference-manager export.

## Features that are not sufficient differentiation

- a PDF/reference sidebar
- retraction counters
- expression-of-concern and correction indicators
- PubPeer availability
- DOI verification
- predatory-journal warnings alone
- preregistration detection
- citation and journal metrics
- saving to Zotero

## Defensible differentiation

The project should compete on properties that are visible, testable, and difficult to bolt onto an opaque score:

1. Exact evidence provenance for every warning.
2. Complete event timelines, including reinstatement and supersession.
3. Separate article, journal, publisher, and claim scopes.
4. Historical date ranges, acquisitions, and exclusions.
5. DOI/ISSN/domain identity resolution before title matching.
6. Local-first privacy with no analytics or anonymous browsing identifier.
7. Open source, reproducible releases, public schemas, and signed data bundles.
8. Native Zotero library and manuscript-bibliography workflows rather than only export.
9. A correction, appeal, and false-positive process.
10. Coverage reporting that distinguishes checked, deferred, unresolved, and failed references.

## Benchmark fixture plan

Before a generalized public launch, compare both products on a fixed public fixture set containing:

- current retractions
- expressions of concern later superseded by retraction
- retractions later reinstated
- corrections without unreliability implications
- duplicate-publication findings
- fabricated DOI and DOI/title mismatch cases
- hijacked journals sharing a title with a legitimate venue
- publishers with mixed journal-level records
- historical ownership changes
- false positives and explicit exclusions
- pages with more than 100 references
- pages where only PMID or PMCID is available

The benchmark should score detection, false positives, source transparency, event chronology, coverage disclosure, privacy behavior, and accessibility. Store screenshots are not enough evidence of runtime behavior.
